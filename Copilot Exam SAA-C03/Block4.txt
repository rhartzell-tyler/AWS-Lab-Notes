Question 44  
A company runs a real-time analytics application using Amazon Kinesis Data Streams. The application must process each record exactly once and must handle sudden spikes in traffic. The company wants to minimize operational overhead. What should a solutions architect recommend?

A. Use Lambda as a consumer with on-demand capacity
B. Use Kinesis Data Firehose with an S3 destination
C. Use Kinesis Client Library (KCL) applications running on EC2
D. Use enhanced fan-out consumers with Lambda

❌ Question 44 — Correct: D
Your answer: A

Why D is correct
For Kinesis Data Streams with:
exactly-once processing
high throughput
sudden spikes
minimal ops
Enhanced fan-out + Lambda is the exam’s preferred answer.
Enhanced fan-out gives each consumer its own 2 MB/s pipe — no shared throughput.

Why A is wrong
Lambda can be a consumer, but without enhanced fan-out it shares shard throughput and can lag.

Question 50  
A company runs a latency-sensitive application on EC2 instances in a single AZ. The company wants to improve availability but must keep latency between the application and its RDS database as low as possible. What should a solutions architect recommend?

A. Deploy EC2 instances across multiple AZs and enable RDS Multi-AZ
B. Keep EC2 instances in one AZ and enable RDS Multi-AZ
C. Deploy EC2 instances across multiple AZs and use RDS read replicas
D. Deploy EC2 instances in one AZ and use RDS read replicas

❌ Question 50 — Correct: B
Your answer: D

Why B is correct
This is the same pattern you saw earlier:
Latency-sensitive app
EC2 + RDS
Must keep app + DB in same AZ
Still want HA
Keep EC2 in one AZ + enable RDS Multi-AZ.

Why D is wrong
Read replicas do not provide HA and do not reduce write latency.

Question 55  
A company runs a high-throughput application that writes data to DynamoDB. The company wants to reduce costs while maintaining high performance. The workload is predictable and steady. What should a solutions architect recommend?

A. Use on-demand capacity mode
B. Use provisioned capacity with Auto Scaling
C. Use DynamoDB Accelerator (DAX)
D. Use DynamoDB Streams

❌ Question 55 — Correct: B
Your answer: A

Why B is correct
For DynamoDB with:
predictable workload
steady throughput
desire to reduce cost
Provisioned capacity with Auto Scaling is the cheapest option.

Why A is wrong
On-demand is great for spiky workloads — but it’s more expensive for steady workloads.
