Question 62  
A company needs to ingest large files (up to 50 GB) from external partners. The partners must upload files securely without gaining access to other objects in the bucket. The company wants to minimize operational overhead. What should a solutions architect recommend?

A. Provide pre-signed S3 URLs
B. Use S3 Access Points
C. Use AWS Transfer Family
D. Use an API Gateway endpoint backed by Lambda

❌ Question 62 — Correct: A
Your answer: B

Why A is correct
For external partners uploading large files (up to 50 GB):
Pre-signed S3 URLs
No credentials needed
Least operational overhead
Partners can upload directly
Automatically scoped to a single object
No access to the rest of the bucket
This is the exam’s preferred pattern.

Why B is wrong
S3 Access Points are great for internal multi-tenant access, but:
They do not isolate uploads per object
They require IAM policies
They are not the simplest solution for external partners
The exam always chooses pre-signed URLs for external uploads unless there’s a compliance or protocol requirement (SFTP, FTPS).

Question 63  
A company runs a data processing pipeline using Amazon SQS and Lambda. Occasionally, Lambda concurrency is exhausted, causing delays. The company wants to ensure that messages are processed reliably even during traffic spikes. What should a solutions architect recommend?

A. Increase Lambda reserved concurrency
B. Use SQS FIFO queues
C. Use SQS → Lambda → SQS dead-letter queue
D. Use SQS → Lambda → Kinesis Data Streams

❌ Question 63 — Correct: A
Your answer: B

Why A is correct
When Lambda concurrency is exhausted:
Increase Lambda reserved concurrency
Guarantees capacity
Prevents throttling
Ensures SQS messages continue flowing
This is the exam’s go-to fix.

Why B is wrong
FIFO queues do not solve concurrency exhaustion.
They actually reduce throughput because:
FIFO = ordered
FIFO = limited parallelism
FIFO = slower
This is a classic AWS trap.
